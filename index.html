My UX Design Process

I use design thinking to leverage ideas and opportunities to solve problems. Since it is an iterative process, it enables us to traverse the design flow and ensure that we take a calculated measurement to deliver the best possible solutions to the problems.

In the process, as a designer, I need to sketch wireframes, build prototypes and mock-ups. I categorize all of these in certain sections of the design thinking process. Everything is listed in detail below.

Empathize

Empathy is the ability to observe and understand another person’s needs, motivations, fears, abilities, limitations, and goals. We can improve users’ experience by removing unnecessary friction or pain.

In UX design, I qualitative research methods of interviewing a diverse set of users by asking open-ended questions which enables me to enlist user behavior, motivations, and needs.

Define

I analyze the observations made during the empathize phase and synthesize them to define the core problems faced by the users. I frame the goal statements, hypothesis statements, and problem statements in this phase.

Ideate

Voila! This is the phase I like the most! It gets exciting here since I dive into building wireframes and low-fidelity mock-ups. In this phase, I brainstorm a lot using various techniques such as crazy-8 ideation(or rapid ideation), round-robin brainstorming(with a team), and lethargy brainstorming(I made this up! It’s when I sleep and think a lot. Believe me, it’s one of the coolest techniques!!!).

Prototype

In this phase, I convert the low-fidelity mock-ups to high-fidelity mock-ups and, possibly turn the high-fidelity mock-ups into intuitive prototypes having interactions between various screens. I generally use Adobe XD and Figma for this purpose.

Test

Testing is an important aspect of the design thinking process. It enables us to understand the product design to a deeper extent after receiving user feedback. I generally test the prototypes with the target users.
